# 🍎 Nutrition and Hydration

Welcome to the **Nutrition and Hydration** section of the 5K Race Preparation Knowledge Base.

Running requires energy, and training also creates a need for consistent nutrition, hydration, and recovery. Preparing for a 5K does not require an overly complicated diet or expensive sports products, but dependable eating and drinking habits can support both training and race preparation.

The goal is to build habits during training that can also be used confidently on race day.

> Race-day nutrition should feel familiar because it has already been practiced during training.

## 🧩 Building a Simple Nutrition Strategy

A practical approach to running nutrition does not need to be complicated.

A runner can focus on:

1. Eating consistently throughout the week
    
2. Including a variety of foods
    
3. Arriving at important workouts adequately fueled
    
4. Maintaining regular hydration
    
5. Eating balanced meals after demanding training
    
6. Practicing race-day food choices before competition
    

The exact amount and timing of food will vary between runners.

What matters most is developing a routine that supports training and can be repeated consistently.

## ⚡ Matching Fuel to the Workout

Different workouts create different energy demands.

A short [[2.weekly-training-plan/easy-run-days|Easy Run Day]] may require less preparation than a demanding speed session or longer run.

More challenging workouts may include:

- [[3.speed-workouts/interval-training|Interval Training]]
    
- [[3.speed-workouts/tempo-runs|Tempo Runs]]
    
- [[2.weekly-training-plan/long-run-day|Long Run Days]]
    
- [[4.strength-and-mobility/strength-training-for-runners|Strength Training]]
    

The goal is not necessarily to eat a large meal before every difficult workout. Instead, runners should avoid consistently beginning important sessions with very low energy.

> Fuel should support the workout rather than become another obstacle to completing it.

## 💧 Hydration and Training Conditions

Hydration needs can change depending on the environment.

Hot or humid weather can increase sweating and make a normal running pace feel more difficult.

During warmer conditions, runners may need to consider:

- Running earlier or later in the day
    
- Carrying water
    
- Reducing pace
    
- Taking additional breaks
    
- Replacing fluids after training
    

The [[6.nutrition-and-hydration/hydration-for-runners|Hydration for Runners]] page explains these ideas in greater detail.

## 🏁 Practice Before Race Day

Race morning should not be the first time a runner experiments with food or hydration.

Training provides an opportunity to learn:

- Which foods feel comfortable before running
    
- How much time is needed between eating and running
    
- How hydration needs change with weather
    
- What meals support recovery
    
- Which routine feels easiest to repeat
    

These habits become especially important during [[7.race-day-strategy/race-morning-preparation|Race Morning Preparation]].

A familiar routine can reduce uncertainty and help the runner focus on the race itself.
