# Training Overview

A weekly training plan is the foundation of preparing for a 5K race because it gives every workout a clear purpose and track progression. Instead of running randomly whenever there is free time, a plan helps organize easy runs, faster workouts, longer runs, and recovery days into one balanced schedule. This matters because a 5K is short enough to require speed, but long enough to demand endurance and consistency.

![A runner using a weekly training schedule to prepare for a 5K race.](5k-training-plan.jpg)

## Why a Weekly Plan Matters

A good 5K plan should not feel like every day is a maximum effort. Some days should build endurance, some should improve speed, and some should let the body recover. For beginners or returning runners, the main goal is to build consistency without getting injured for example shin splits. For more experienced runners, the goal may be improving pace, running stronger finishes, or feeling more confident on your training on race day.

### Main Parts of the Week

A complete weekly plan usually includes:

1. Easy run days
2. One speed workout
3. One longer run
4. Strength or mobility work
5. Recovery days
