# Rest and Recovery

Rest and recovery are often overlooked in a 5K training plan, but they are just as important as the actual workouts. Training breaks the body down slightly, and recovery is when the body adapts and becomes stronger. Without recovery, a runner may feel tired all the time, lose motivation, or in the worst and most likely case, increase the risk of an easily avoidable injury.

## Why Recovery Matters

Recovery gives the muscles, joints, and nervous system time to handle the stress of running. This is especially important when the week includes harder sessions like [[speed-workouts|speed workouts]] or a [[long-run-day|long run day]]. A runner who never rests may be working hard, but that does not always mean they are improving. Progress usually comes from balancing effort with enough sleep, nutrition, stretching, and lighter movement.

### Recovery Habits

Good recovery can include simple habits such as:

- Sleeping enough each night,7-8 hours of sleep
- Drinking enough water, preferably a gallon of water a day 
- Eating balanced meals, don't eat overly greasy foods
- Stretching tight areas
- Taking easy days seriously, they are easy for a reason don't try to push it
- Listening to pain before it becomes an injury

Rest days also connect back to the [[weekly-training-plan/training-overview|training overview]] because a strong weekly plan should include both work and recovery. The goal is not just to train hard for one week, but to stay consistent long enough to be ready for race day.
